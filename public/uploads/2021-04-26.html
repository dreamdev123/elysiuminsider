ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 26th of APRIL 2021


The Past & Highlights

The stock market was up-and-down last week. Overall trading volumes was relatively light. Small-caps outperformed larger-caps slightly and the technology-heavy Nasdaq Composite Index modestly lagged the broad market. The impression was that the stock market traded without a clear theme, for example what stocks would benefit most from reopening or any clear rotation between sectors. Only sector that was clearly weaker was semiconductors. The main trigger during the week was US President Joe Biden’s plan to propose a close to doubling of the long-term capital gains tax rate for taxpayers who earn more than USD 1 million a year. This pressured stocks and most asset-classes sharply during Thursday’s trading session. The generated tax income is expected to be used for free community college tuition, child-care subsidies, and other health- or education-related spending. This is expected to be a part of Biden’s American Families Plan that should formally be proposed this week. The stock market recovered some of the intraday loss when they realized that the that negotiations in Congress would likely make any final tax increase lower than what Biden initially proposes.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The negative sentiment surrounding the US dollar persisted and the US dollar was the weakest currency among the majors last week. While Euro and Yen (JPY) was the strongest currencies among the majors last week. Aussie dollar (AUD) was the second weakest while the kiwi dollar (NZD) was the third strongest.  So was a mixed week overall. 

The EURUSD pair continued to increase for another consecutive week, this past week by approximately 1,1% and are now trading very close to our price objective for the first wave (1?). It certainly looks like it should be viewed as the first wave (1?) upwards in our view, where there after most likely should follow a smaller corrective downwards second wave (2?). Forward looking, the break of the high point of wave one (1?) after that the correction downwards wave two (2?) is finalized would then confirm that we in theory should be in the third wave (3?) in a sequence of five ways, where the third one should be the longest bullish wave in the sequence of the five waves for EURUSD, in a theoretic Eliot Wave count.

Commodities 
 
Spot gold (XAUUSD) moved lower on Friday and closed unchanged for the week. A major resistance for the precious metal is seen near the February highs around 1855 USD/ounce. It is little difficult in the short term to clearly see where gold is heading this week since the somewhat negative end to last week made that the MACD-indicator lost a little momentum in the histogram which is a little leading bearish indicator. Meanwhile MACD overall still is in buy territory and the bottom line is that gold is value at this levels for longer-term value investors.  



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart with MACD & EMA 50/200
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) started the week upwards and traded around 64500 USD during Wednesday, which was the intra-week high. Price consolidated through the week and slowly pulled back close to the week’s starting point around 60000 USD on Saturday. During early Sunday we saw a sharper fall mainly due to heavy Asian selling. The price dropped from 60000 USD to 54148 USD in around 15 minutes. Exactly why the fast price dropped occurred is not established but could be anything from rumors on that the US Treasury is planning to charge several financial institutions for money laundering using cryptocurrencies or that CNBC tweeted a one-month-old report that India preparing a ban on Bitcoin and other crypto currencies, and speculations that the partial crypto ban in Turkey might trigger other countries to do the same. But comparing to the overall performance of Bitcoins the price drop is very small, looked by many longer-term investors as a “buy on dips” opportunity.
