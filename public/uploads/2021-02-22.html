ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 22nd of FEBRUARY 2021


The Past & Highlights

Initially the US Stock market set new highs last week, but then the stock market bounced-back during the remaining of the week. Focus was the Congressional hearings aimed at deciphering the recent GameStop trading frenzy, ongoing Washington negotiations over the details of the coming fiscal-aid package and how the lockdowns have impacted economic-data. 

But the main trigger for the bearish sentiment for the equity market was the upward move in the longer-term interest rates, the US 10-year treasury bill increase to 1,3%, German 10-year bund yield at -0,3%, UK 10-year gilt yield at 0,7%, and Japan 10-year JGB yield was at 0,1%. These benchmark yields are either already back at pre-pandemic levels or closing in to.

But overall, the fundamental outlook remains quite favorable because interest rates increase for a good reason. The inflation expectations have doubled since last year low and are now close to the levels seen 2013. US Retail sales showed that spending increased by 5,8% in January relatively to January 2020. Together all this indicates a strong GDP come-back in the second half of the year. 

After all, the key factor is the development of the pandemic. And statistics indicates that cases moving lower globally, as more vaccine doses are delivered to help speed up roll-out in Europe. Top performing with regards to vaccinations are Israel, leading with close to 80% (of the population has received their first shot). The US is currently around 17%. The UK is ahead of Europe with nearly 25% while EU due to slow vaccine rollout and lack of supply have vaccinated around 5,2% of the population. It is likely we see a gradual easing of restrictions as the weather gets warmer in the coming weeks and months.  This is very supporting for the economic activity.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
Equity markets took a minor time-out the past week while focus shifted to rising industrial metal prices and longer-term treasury yields. The bullish metal prices are explained more by supply shortage than a hefty increase in demand.   As a result, the strong metal prices boosted Australian Dollar (AUD) to be the best performer, followed by Sterling (GBP) companied by other “commodity” currencies. On the other hand, Swiss Franc (CHF) and Yen (JPY) ended as the worst performing ones among the major currencies. US dollar and Euro were also relatively weak, but slightly stronger performing than Swiss franc and Yen.
EURUSD did meet the resistance from the bottom of the former trading channel (mentioned last week) and we saw another wave to the downside during the middle of the week. The pair reversed around the 1,2030 area and appear now to be slowly trending upwards again. Likely to test the resistance line around1,22 during this week.

Commodities 
 
Gold spot (XAUUSD) fall last week as the precious metal touch an eight-month low. Gold was under some pressure due to the steep rise in US Treasury yields and a relatively firm dollar. The main drivers behind the pressure were optimism over a larger economic stimulus package and the somewhat successful corona vaccination roll-out.

Meanwhile buyers started to show up and the selling pressure began to subside a little as the market once again tested a major 50% to 61,8% retracement zone at USD 1787,30 to 1711,70.  For longer-term investors, this price level represents value so we could see a short-term support base form over the near-term.

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) rallied by around 18% in the week ending 21st of February. Following on from a 25% breakout from the previous week, Bitcoin ended the week at 57 376 USD. Monday was the intraweek low when price hit around 45 730 USD, well above the first main support level at 41 134 USD. The intra-week high was 58 321 USD during the weekend trading session on Sunday.
