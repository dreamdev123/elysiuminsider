ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 19th of APRIL 2021


The Past & Highlights

Stock market, bonds and commodities all had a strong week. Dow Jones traded above 34 000 for the first time, all supported by strong macro-economic data releases and re-openings in developed countries. Consumer spending data indicates a rise in demand, inflation data indicates stronger demand and pricing power, and the upwards earning indicates a strong recovery. Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The US dollar was weak during the past week, pressured by falling treasury yield while US and German stocks set new All-Time-Highs. Canadian Dollar (CAD) was the second weakest among the major currencies followed by Swiss Franc (CHF). The risk on sentiment favored New Zealand dollar (NZD) and the Aussie dollar (AUD) which where the strongest among the majors. Euro and Japanese yen (JPY) hand a more mixed performance trough out the past week. 
 
The EURUSD pair continued to increase for another consecutive week, again by approximately 1,5%. So certainly, it was a new wave that was formed but now probably are in its end phase. If followed by a smaller wave down it would indicate that the A-B-C correction was complete, and we might enter a new five wave cycle upwards.

Commodities 
 
Spot gold (XAUUSD) had a strong week and traded at its highest level in seven weeks during Friday. The best weekly performance since the middle of December. The main trigger was the sharp decline in US treasury yields on Thursday in combination with a weak US dollar. Gold looks very interesting now and tests the upper line of the falling trading channel from august. We also see support from the MACD indicator and that the slope of EMA50 (Exponential Moving Average) now is upwards. All together gold might be more in fashion the next month. We just wait for confirmation of the longer trend, i.e., EMA50 crosses EMA200 from below. 



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart with MACD & EMA 50/200
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) started the week upwards and traded around 64500 USD during Wednesday, which was the intra-week high. Price consolidated through the week and slowly pulled back close to the week’s starting point around 60000 USD on Saturday. During early Sunday we saw a sharper fall mainly due to heavy Asian selling. The price dropped from 60000 USD to 54148 USD in around 15 minutes. Exactly why the fast price dropped occurred is not established but could be anything from rumors on that the US Treasury is planning to charge several financial institutions for money laundering using cryptocurrencies or that CNBC tweeted a one-month-old report that India preparing a ban on Bitcoin and other crypto currencies, and speculations that the partial crypto ban in Turkey might trigger other countries to do the same. But comparing to the overall performance of Bitcoins the price drop is very small, looked by many longer-term investors as a “buy on dips” opportunity.
