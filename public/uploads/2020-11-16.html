ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 16th of NOVEMBER 2020
 

The Past & Highlights

In US, the S&P 500 closed at a new all-time-high and global equities had a second week of gains following news of progress in developing a COVID-19 vaccine. Equities was particularly strengthen the past Monday after Pfizer and BioNTech announced that their vaccine had a 90% effectiveness ratio in their study, triggering a wave of hope and optimism that a medical solution will address the health crisis and accelerate the economic recovery. 
 
Cyclical stocks that previously had been more negatively impacted by corona was among the best performers last week while sectors that have benefited from the pandemic underperformed. Small-cap generally outperformed US large-caps. The vaccine news was indeed good news, but also to some degree expected by the financial markets and this can probably lead to a more sustainable recovery during next year but normalization from the pandemic is probably expected first by fall 2021. But very likely we can expect more near-term uncertainties that triggers volatility. 



S&P 500 daily chart
 

Outlook

The US Presidential Elections is left behind us and financial markets will shift attention to the development of the pandemic and the new November 19th Brexit “deadline” for negotiators to come up with a trade deal.  Investors are hoping a deal will be in place so it can be present to EU leaders during their video conference on Thursday.  Any Corona vaccine will not be widely available until earliest by the spring and daily case rates are still increasing as we approach the winter month, bringing with it more restrictions and lockdowns worldwide. This can trigger a lot of market volatility since lockdowns delays any economic recovery substantially.

Main contributions were from EURJPY followed by NZDUSD. But most pairs contributed with positive returns exempt for the GBPJPY and GBPNZD crosses, for the current month.

Currency Markets
 
As for all financial markets, the news of the coronavirus vaccine was the main market mover also for forex exchange markets last week. It was a clear risk-on sentiment all week. Swiss Franc (CHF) and Yen (JPY) ended as the worst performing ones, followed by Canadian Dollar (CAD), which was dragged down by the pull back in oil prices. New Zealand Dollar (NZD), Australian Dollar (AUD) and Sterling (GBP) was among the strongest currencies. Dollar (USD) and Euro (EUR) was somewhat mixed. 

EURUSD is back in its trading channel and trading in a longer consolidation phase between 1,16 – 1,20 area.

Commodities 
 
XAUUSD spot gold tested the downside support around 1847 USD/ounce area during last Monday’s trading session on the corona vaccine news and has since then slowly traded higher. Since support did not break, and its bounced slightly prior to the support it can be viewed as slightly bullish indication longer term.
