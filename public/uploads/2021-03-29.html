Newsletter - Portfolio Journal - March 29, 2021

ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 29th of MARCH 2021


The Past & Highlights

Stock markets was overall slightly up last week. Investors was going back and forward between optimism about reopening and concerns about increase in inflation and interest rates. Therefore, the lack of clear direction. Small cap underperformed for the second consecutive week, and in S&P500 especially communication services declined.

Among the headlines was the temporarily closure of the Suez Canal because of a grounded cargo ship. It raised concerns with regards to global supply lines but boosted oil prices and energy stocks. The signals of fighting of the corona pandemic appears to be a major theme in the financial markets. During the start of the past week investors seemed encouraged by AstraZeneca’s release of data showing its vaccine was highly effective and safe in US trials. Then the pharmaceutical company revealed revised results on Wednesday showing that the vaccine was slightly less effective at staving off infection but still 100% effective in trials at preventing severe disease and hospitalization. The stock market was also pressured from increasing corona infection numbers in US as well as renewed lockdowns in Europe. But year to date (YTD) S&P500 is still up around 6 % but if we look one year back S&P500 is up around 50%. So, it is still fair to say that US equities are in a bull market that started the 23ed of march which was the 2020 low.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The past week started in a cautious mode but by the end of last week the risk sentiment turned and stocks went up. In the forex market Aussie dollar (AUD), New Zeeland dollar (NZD) and Sterling (GBP) came back strong by the end of the week. Japanese Yen (JPY), Swiss Franc (CHF) was heavily sold off by the end of the week. All in line with the common risk appetite patterns. But all together the USD was the best performer among the majors last week, followed by Canadian dollar (CAD) supported by the increase in oil prices, while Aussie and New Zealand dollars was the worst performers. 

EURUSD, following up regarding the wave count it looks like the downwards A-B-C correction from the longer raising trend might be finalized soon. If is not an exemption or the start of a change in the longer-term trend. So, it is not confirmed that the current downwards wave is finalized yet, but it looks likely that we soon might see a wave upwards from these levels.

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) had the US dollar against it the past week but closed higher on Friday after the 10-year US Treasury yield and the US Dollar Index pulled back from their intraday highs. Gold has overall been moving mostly sideways to lower all week with investors buying dips and selling rallies. In support of the gold price was rising coronavirus cases and lockdowns in Europe as well as the US Federal Reserve’s loose policy.



XAUUSD daily chart (with MACD-indicator)
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) had a volatile week starting of bearish during the beginning of the week and then turned the other direction. Intra-week-low was around 50 600 USD during Thursday’s trading session, then the crypto started to trend upwards the remaining of the week and traded around 56 000 over the weekend. During Monday BTCUSD made a steep increase and now trades around 58 000 USD. 

Major Investment Bank Morgan Stanley is reported to be the first major player to open access to Bitcoin funds for high-net-worth investors during this week. Others are likely to follow so demand for Bitcoin is likely to increase this year.
