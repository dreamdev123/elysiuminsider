ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 4th of JANUARY 2021


The Past & Highlights

It was another short week and the S&P500 set a new fresh record high on Monday as another round of unemployment benefits and stimulus checks were announced. Focus was on the size of the stimulus package and the number of new corona cases and the progress of the initial vaccine rollout. In England 75 percent of the population are now affected by the country’s toughest coronavirus restrictions. Finally, EU and UK signed a Brexit deal which was very positive for the financial markets. With regards to macro data initial jobless claims in US that were sizably below consensus estimates and the prior period. Although positive, the levels are still firmly elevated from pre-pandemic levels and point to a job market that is still under stress. As we enter the new year, it is likely to expect GDP growth to slow in US during Q1, but eventually recovering.



S&P500 daily chart
 

Outlook

Risk appetite will fluctuate with the number of new corona cases reported and the success of the vaccination plan. So, a highly volatile year is expected. But the markets will overall be supported by the large monetary and fiscal measures that has been rolled-out. This will most likely have a positive long-term effect on risk appetite going forward. Generally global economies get boosted by the shift into a post-vaccine phase, and unemployment will slowly start to fall back supported by consumer consumption.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
UK quietly said farewell to the EU on January the 1st without major incidents. We might have to wait a couple of weeks to see if large numbers of trucks back up on both sides of the English Channel, or if supermarkets start running out of fresh fruits. Most likely the more short-term worst scenarios for GBP will not occur.  

USDJPY is currently trading close to it’s support around 103,00 today, with Covid-19 restrictions from the central Japanese government, if they occur, they could potentially trigger more yen repatriation flows. USDJPY could potentially then fall to 101,00 area in the coming weeks.
 


USDJPY daily chart
 

Commodities & Cryptos 
 
Gold’s (XAUUSD) bearish correction could be over. Gold has been in a negative consolidation since beginning of July, but gold got a very strong start of the year and have now broken out of the negative trading channel. The fast vaccine progress globally has reduced demand for safe havens, but now since the magnitude of the second wave are larger than anticipated gold has come in fashion again. The outlook for gold looks very attractive for 2021 since Biden’s administration probably means trillions of fiscal stimulus, and gold is one of the best ways to preserve the value against potential inflation.  The similar concept also applies to Bitcoin (BTC) that now trades at a record high above 31 000 USD.
