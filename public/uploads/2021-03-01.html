Newsletter - Portfolio Journal - March 1, 2021

ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 1st of MARCH 2021


The Past & Highlights

Investors focused on interest rates last week, as the rise in government bond yields accelerated, worrying both equity and fixed-income markets. The volatility in the bond market increased to the highest-level since April last year. Yields are still on very low levels but have continued to increase higher over the past two months due to the increased likelihood of an economic boom, or a rapid V-shaped recovery with accelerating inflation occurring later this year. During the semiannual testimony to Congress on Wednesday, Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell stated that “higher yields are a statement of confidence about the recovery”
 

Outlook

The prospects for growth have increased over the past couple of months. The V-shaped recovery is considered increasingly likely to become true. US President Biden’s 1,9 trillion USD stimulus package, if approved, will hit the economy simultaneously as vaccines are distributed and the economy reopens, boosting economic growth. All combined, the GDP growth in 2021 can be the fastest growth pace in the last 35 years, exceeding 5% and supporting a rise in inflation and interest rates. 

Therefore, equity valuations can start to normalize as corporate profits recover. Currently around 95% of the S&P500 companies have reported fourth-quarter earnings.  The reports so far have overall beaten estimates and are now growing after three consecutive quarters of year-over-year (YOY) declines. S&P500 analyst estimates for 2021 have been revised upwards by approximately 4% since the start of the year.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The US Dow Jones Index hit a record high in the middle of the past week, and then the rally was stalled by the hefty increase in treasury yields. The USD was the strongest currency of the majors last week followed by EURO and the sterling (GBP). On the other side was the Aussie Dollar (AUD) as it reversed some of the month’s gains, followed by Swiss Franc (CHF) and Japanese Yen (JPY). Price movements might have been exaggerated by month end position adjustments, an affect that might be even larger by the end of March since that also will be the end of the first quarter.

GBPJPY has been very bullish but now reached its ultimate target around 150,000 area, where it looks like it clearly reversed. The resistance was tested but appears to be too strong to break. We see some bearish signals in general in several sterling (GBP) pairs and it looks like it has been over bought and we might see some downside corrections. In the first wave it is likely to see GBPJPY bounce to the 145,000 area.

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) had a volatile week, starting bullish in the early week but to decline during Wednesday’s trading session. Gold was negatively impacted by FED’s Jeromy Powell’s speech, resulting in increasing bond yields triggering the US dollar to become the asset of choice, short-term driving down foreign demand for dollar-denominated gold. 

The real test for gold is the EMA 50 (Exponential Moving Average) narrowing down on the EMA 200. In its simplest form of Technical Analysis, the long trend is still up since the lines have not yet crossed. If no crossing, there might be a good set up to long in gold around these levels.



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) felled by around 21% in the week ending 28th of February. Reversing the 18% increase from the previous week, Bitcoin ended the week at 45 300 USD. The recall in Bitcoin is most likely explained by concerns that rising US Treasury bond yields might prompt the Federal Reserve to tighten monetary policy sooner rather than later to keep inflation from rising out of control. Traders and analysts have indicated that such a move could initiate a short-term sell-off in more risky assets including stocks and bitcoin. On the other hand, the main function of Bitcoin is to preserve value, therefore many investors and traders sees this as temporarily and an opportunity to scale in. Another important Bitcoin news presented last week is that JPMorgan, the biggest US bank, stated that investors could allocate 1% of their portfolios into the largest cryptocurrency (Bitcoin). All together this looks more like a buy on dip scenario, a recall from the rally we have seen lately.
