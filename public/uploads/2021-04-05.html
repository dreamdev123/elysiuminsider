ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 5th of APRIL 2021


The Past & Highlights

Eastern holiday made the week short, and we can summarize a strong first quarter for the stock market. S&P500 traded above 4000 for the first-time during Thursday. Among major events was consumer confidence, that increased to its highest level since the start of the pandemic, and manufacturing activity expanding in March at the fastest pace since 1983. Another major happening was President Biden's sweeping infrastructure proposal. The president’s plan is named “American Jobs Plan” and proposes a 2,25 trillion USD in investments infrastructure, manufacturing, research and development, and clean energy, among others over an eight-year period. That is probably the first of a two-part recovery package that could approach 4 trillion USD in total, with the second part focusing on social spending, like universal pre-kindergarten and national childcare.

If the execution of the plan is successful and spending leads to a stronger labor market, improved competitiveness, technological advances, and productivity gains the plan could help supporting above-average growth levels and provide significant benefits to the US economy over a longer time frame.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The eastern holiday has made the week shorter than normal. EUR and the US dollar has been relatively weak trough out the weak while sterling (GBP) has been the strongest among the majors. Canadian dollar (CAD), Kiwi dollar (NZD) and the Aussie dollar (AUD) as all been very strong so the conclusion is that traders had a strong risk appetite through the week and in the close of the first quarter. 

EURUSD, no change in view from last week. In the wave count it still looks like the downwards A-B-C correction from the longer raising trend might be finalized soon. We can even see that the price has started to consolidate through the past week.  If is not an exemption or the start of a change in the longer-term trend. So, it is still not confirmed that the current downwards wave is finalized yet, but it looks likely that we should continue in a wave upwards from these levels. But will probably be decided within the course of the week.

Commodities 
 
Was a thin volume for gold ahead of the long Easter holiday weekend. Overall gold futures closed slightly lower over the week. Higher interest yields are now already incorporated in gold prices and investors also anticipate that rates can continue to increase in-line with inflation and the development of the economy. So, it is not unlikely that the focus on yields vs. gold price can decouple somewhat. This could ease of some of the pressure we have seen on god recently. 

Spot gold (XAUUSD) looks very interesting from a technical point of view. We see the double bottom formation around 1676,50 USD/Ounce which many times is a confirmation of a change in a price trend. In a first move it can be expected that gold could trade upwards towards 1775 and thereafter decide to break through the falling trend or remain in the negative trend that started in august last year.

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) started the week bullish and increased from around 55 000 USD in the Monday morning session to around 58 000 USD. Then more slowly increased trough out the week up to around 59 800 USD during Friday which was the intra-week high.  The crypto then pulled back a little during the thinner trading during the extended Eastern Holliday Weekend. 

When summarizing the first quarter of 2021, it is now calculated that the crypto industry received more funding in the first quarter of the year than during the whole 2020. According to Bloomberg who has summarized data from business analytics firm CB Insights, 129 startups focusing on crypto and blockchain technology have raised 2,6 billion in the first quarter of this year. Another confirmation of the breakthrough on a broader basis for the crypto industry that many analyst believe we could experience this year.
