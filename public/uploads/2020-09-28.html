ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 28th of SEPTEMBER 2020
 

The Past & Highlights
 
 
Stock market fell last week, marking the longest weekly slide since 2019, as traders continue to digest news that U.S./China trade tensions are rising, and coronavirus vaccine won't be widely available until April of 2021. Also, jobs data came out worse than expected, and expectations are fading that a new fiscal stimulus package will be passed.

The large blue-chip stocks outperformed the S&P 500 last week, but Microsoft, Apple, Netflix, Amazon and Facebook are all still down for the month. Small-cap and cyclical stocks responded worse to the news that a vaccine is further away than initially thought. The death of Ruth Bader Ginsburg's has resulted in fears that stimulus talks between Republicans and Democrats could be overshadowed by a political battle for a Supreme Court nominee. The large surge in coronavirus cases in Europe has also impacted stock markets in Europe as they fear new lock-downs that would delay an economic recovery.
 

EA Portfolio Performance

Elysium AURUM DIGITAL portfolio continued to perform during the past week. A total net return of around 3,09% (adjusted for performance fees) for September, month to date. Main contributions were from EURCAD followed by USDJPY, but all pairs contributed with positive returns so far for the current month. The portfolio now also actively trades in gold (XAUUSD) which also contributed with positive returns during the past week.

In September the portfolio mainly traded USDCAD and GBPUSD, representing around 25 percent of the trades each followed by USDJPY of around 19%. Other pairs traded frequently was EURUSD and GBPJPY. Best risk/reward attribution was from EURUSD followed by GBPJPY so far for the current month. 

Elysium ALPHA LINEA portfolio has mostly traded the majors EURUSD followed by the GBPUSD pair in September. The portfolio has a net increase by around 6,44% (adjusted for performance fees) in September so far.
 

Currency Markets

USD, the recent growing turmoil in the world's stock markets has led investors to take a more cautious stance on riskier assets. In the foreign exchange market, investors prefer to resort to the dollar. But the weekly commitment of trader’s report covering the week until September 22 found that speculators increased their net dollar short against ten IMM currency futures. This during a week where the dollar outperformed against most major currencies exempt from the JPY. Increasing net longs in euro, Swiss franc and the Japanese yen helped boost total short dollar bets by 9% to USD 34,9 billion.
 
EURUSD 30 minutes 5 days chart
 

GBP went up in the early European Monday session and pushed the EURGBP near the three-week lows. 

EURGBP extended last week's retracement slide from the 0,9215-20 area and faced heavy selling for the fourth consecutive session. The streaght of the pound relative to the euro is most likely a consequence of reports that both the UK and the EU made key concessions to avoid a no-deal Brexit. 

Traders believe that the two sides remain optimistic that some kind of a deal will be struck before the very important EU summit in mid-October. A final round of BREXIT negotiations is scheduled for Tuesday which could be a trigger for short-covering of the GBP.
 
GBPUSD 30 minutes 5 days chart
 
 
Commodities 

Gold (XAUUSD) and Silver (XAGUSD) are currently challenged by the risk of further dollar strength and current bond yield developments where a steady nominal yields disguising falling break evens (inflation expectations) and rising real yields. The overall holdings of ETF’s backed by bullion is steady but it has been a small decline since last Monday’s surge while traders in the week to September 22 reduced bullish bets to near the lowest in 15 months. This looks like a response to the technical outlook which in the short term are challenged following the break of the triangle formation and below key support of $1900/oz. Gold has support at $1837/oz, which is the 38.2% retracement of the March to August rally. The U.S. presidential debate, an emerging EM debtorries and the European Banking index trading at a record low all provides fundamental support for gold going forward.
