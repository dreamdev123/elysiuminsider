ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 5th of OCTOBER 2020
 

The Past & Highlights
 
 
Fewer without jobs in the US and focus on Trump

During the past week the manufacturing industry's purchasing managers' index from large parts of the world confirmed, just as expected, that activity remains high and the positive view of the order books indicates that the recovery in the industry will continue in the coming months. However, it will be some time before we make up for the loss of production caused by the Covid-19 pandemic.

Employment in the United States increased by 660,000 people in September, a smaller increase than expected, but last month's figure was revised upwards. Unemployment fell to 7,9 percent from 8,4 percent in August, driven by a reduction in the labor force. The figure confirms the picture that the recovery is losing some of its momentum.

With just one month left until the US presidential election, the news came on Friday that US President Donald Trump and his wife have been hit by Covid-19. They have been quarantined and the president's campaign meetings have been canceled. It is unclear what will happen to the next two presidential debates.
On Wednesday, it's time for the Vice-Presidential debate in the United States. Many hope that the debate will contain more factual politics than last week's presidential debate, which can rather be described as a quarrel.

On the statistical front, the focus is on the current Purchasing Managers' Index for the service sector, which we receive from large parts of the world. Preliminary figures point to a decline in the euro area after the virus spread again and new restrictions was introduced.

Currency Markets

Main focus this week will be Trump health and FOMC minutes and the US Vice Presidential debate between Pence and Harris. The fact that Donald Trump fell ill with Corona controls much of the news flow and it was probably lucky for the financial markets that the most critical phase seems to have occurred over the weekend. The fact that it occasionally felt a little uneasy during the last week was mainly due to the fact that the spread of Covid-19 in Europe continued to rise and that new restrictions have been imposed. However, these have a much smaller scope than before and are also more limited in time. It is estimated in the markets that the negative consequences for the economies will be limited as long as the restrictions can be kept at these levels.

EURUSD is currently trading above its 50 four-hour Simple Moving Average (SMA) and are testing the 100 SMA resistance. Support could be found at 1,1685 from the bounce from last week.

Commodities 

The past week for precious metals, gold and silver was very quiet both in terms of price action and positioning. During Friday both gold (XAUUSD) and silver (XAGUSD) showed a dull response to the news on president Trumps Covid-19 infection. Gold and silver still trades in a consolidation range despite multiple sources of uncertainty. We see some support for gold around USD 1882/oz with a break raising the risk of a deeper correction to the recent low at USD 1850/oz. The longer-term fundamentals are more bullish in our view.
