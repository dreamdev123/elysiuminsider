Newsletter - Portfolio Journal - May 3, 2021

ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 3rd of MAY 2021


The Past & Highlights

Major indexes closed the past week mostly slightly lower, but both S&P500 and NASDAQ set new highs before declining during Friday. Many first-quarter reports were presented and returns within and among sectors varied widely, but oil stocks generally traded up due to the rise in oil prices to a six-week high. Also, communication services stocks outperformed within the S&P500 supported by strong earnings from Facebook and Alphabet (Google). Technology stocks underperformed negatively affected by Microsoft, that fell even due earnings beat estimates. Pharmaceuticals also had a poor week. Approximately 180 of the 500 companies in S&P500 reported last week. Earnings are estimated to have increased by around 30 percent for the first quarter compared to first quarter 2020.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The past week Canadian dollar (CAD) was the currency to own, it was the strongest among the majors. Fundamentally supported by oil prices which resulted in that it traded through some technical resistances and still looks short-term bullish.  Swiss Franc (CHF) ended as the second strongest among the majors. The US dollar (USD) was mostly under pressure the whole week but had a strong rebound in Friday’s trading session. The rise in global treasury yields sent Japanese Yen (JPY) lower to end as the worst performing. The late week sell off in Euro and Pound (GBP) should indicate that Yen could consolidate through this week, but the outlook for Yen is generally more bearish in a slightly longer horizon.

EURUSD, our wave counts so far seem to have been “spot on”. Friday’s sell off in EUR created a break of the upwards support line in wave “1?”. This could then be the beginning of the anticipated downwards correction wave “2?”. Will be interesting to follow this throughout the remaining of the week.

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) was under pressure last week due to the increase in US Treasury yields. The major triggers for the weak gold were a series of better-than-expected US economic reports. They were strong enough to offset the dovish communication from the US Federal Reserve and Fed Chair Jerome Powell. Meanwhile the technical view of gold is that is has lost a little of the momentum the past week and the MACD histogram has fallen back but are still in buy territory. But looks like it could regain some of its momentum this week. Both the Exponential Averages (EMA) 50 and 200 slopes are upwards, we just wait to see a crossing to confirm a longer trend upwards again. Keep in mind that Goldman Sachs say they expects to see gold prices at 2000 USD/ounce within the next six months.
  



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart (with MACD-Indicator)
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) started the past week bullish. Traded up from Monday’s intra-week-low around 47 700 USD in a steady pace. Tuesday and Wednesday were sideways with a small bounce back during Thursday and then going into a rally mode again and trading around 58 535 USD during Friday, which is where the crypto trades now.  There is resistance at 60 000 USD where quite a “few” limit-sell orders are placed. Solid support is found around 52 000 USD. Many times, a large bunch of orders serve as a magnet for traders, so would not be surprised if those will be taken out trough out the week and we establish trading above the 60 000 USD level again.
