ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 15th of FEBRUARY 2021


The Past & Highlights

The US stock market closed the week modestly positive supported by strong earnings growth and the increasing likelihood of additional fiscal stimulus. Vaccine distribution has picked up steam, and the US government has announced plans to purchase an additional 200 million corona vaccines. Inflation and jobs data came in below expectations during last week, as growth has stalled, and jobless claims continue to remain significantly higher than pre-pandemic levels. It is evident that entertainment and restaurant industries largely still are paused but when vaccine campaign is scaled up the job market, entertainment and restaurant business will improve. It is likely that it will get closer to pre-pandemic levels by the end of the year supported by fiscal stimulus, monetary policy, and the gradual easing of restrictions.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The past week US dollar tumbled due to the increase in risk appetite and was the weakest of the major currencies. Instead, the Aussie dollar (AUD) was on the other side of the spectrum and was the strongest among the majors. Other majors were more mixed in their development. Despite the strength in the oil and equity market it did not spill over much into the Canadian dollar (CAD) nor New Zeeland dollar (NZD). Swiss Franc had a strong week while the Japanese Yen (JPY) was very week.  When traders are back from the lunar new year holiday, we might see more volume and clearer direction. 

EURUSD broke out on the upside with clarity and strength from the falling trading channel and almost touched the target area of around 1,2170 mentioned last week. The question is if the pair will break back into the former raising trading channel from September 2020 or if the channel line will continue to serve as a resistance and we will see another wave to the downside.

Commodities 
 
Gold spot (XAUUSD) started the past week strong but fall back during Thursday’s trading session and during Friday the pullback continued. But gold is being supported over the short run by the expectations for a 1,9 trillion US dollar coronavirus relief package, given its status as a hedge against inflation. The market now pricing in an average annual inflation of around 2,23% for the next 10 years. That is actually the highest expected level since 2014. This is fundamentally very supportive for the precious metal. 
Technically gold is balancing on its EMA 200 (Exponential Moving Average). It is an important level that is more likely to hold than break. 



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart with EMA 50/200
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) rallied during Monday the past week due to the news of Tesla’s adoption of Bitcoin as a form of payment for its products in the future. Then Mastercard announced that it will start allowing cardholders to transact in certain cryptocurrencies. Bitcoin had its intra-week high during the weekend when traded around 49 448 USD. Which also was a new all-time-high for the leading cryptocurrency. Overall, the week exempt from Monday’s bull rally was relatively stable. 

During the past week, the first North American bitcoin Exchange Traded Fund (ETF) was approved during Thursday by the Ontario Securities Commission. This might clear the path faster for a US bitcoin ETF. All in all, there was many fundamentally bullish news for the cryptocurrency the past week, which also reflected in the price.
