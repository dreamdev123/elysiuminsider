ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 9thof NOVEMBER 2020
 

The Past & Highlights

Joe Biden has been named the winner by the AP, as expected but the race was probably a lot tighter than the market anticipated. Mr. Biden will then become the 46th president of the United States, even though Trump has not yet admitted defeat. And United States will most likely continue to have a divided Congress with a Democratic majority in the House of Representatives and a Republican in the Senate. But it can be difficult to get a stimulus package in place before the end of January and in the longer term, the split in Congress can, among other things, make it difficult for Biden to get through his tax reform.
 
During the past week, the US central bank Fed maintained its monetary policy, with its statement virtually unchanged. The Fed meeting, which was to a large extent overshadowed by the political turbulence, as well as the large second wave of coronavirus across the country. This is despite the pressure to act soaring in the light of diminishing prospects for aggressive fiscal stimulus.

The FED funds rate was left unchanged at 0,00-0,25% and the central bank said it will continue to purchase treasury securities and agency mortgage-backed securities as previously announced at least at the current pace. In terms of USD this is equivalent to around 120bn per month. The previous forward guidance implemented in September was unchanged, i.e. the policy rate remains tied to a 2% average inflation target. 
 

Outlook

COVID-19 is still considered to be the main downside risk for the global economies. And as long as the virus is around, pressuring the economic development, there will not be a full recovery, according to the Fed, which points out that economic development and employment is continuing its recovery path, but remains well below pre-pandemic levels, and demand stays weak.
 
Also, in Europe the fast resurgence in COVID-19 cases has led to a second wave of lockdowns, all very negative for the recovery of the economies. Geographically the distribution of corona cases is not totally uniform. European lowland countries such as Belgium and Netherlands together with France and Spain have so far been significantly more affected.  So now when the US president election is in the past, focus will shift again more towards the global pandemic. The risk appetite was back on in the start of the week, with equites up and gold down as a result that the presidential election now is finalized.  
 
Pfizer Inc. and BioNTech shares rose in premarket trading when the companies said their mRNA-based vaccine candidate, BNT162b2, against SARS-CoV-2 has demonstrated evidence of efficacy against COVID-19 in participants without prior evidence of SARS-CoV-2 infection. The new data is based on the first interim efficacy analysis conducted 8th of November by an external, independent data-monitoring committee from the Phase 3 clinical study. The vaccine news boosted the risk appetite even more resulting in an increase in the precious metal sell off and crude oil rally.

Currency Markets
 
A weaker USD across the line is the result of the Biden victory and we see a more bullish tendency in high beta currencies such as AUD, NOK, and SEK. The market is positioning for reflation, and the EURUSD tested the 1,19 resistance and USDJPY is back to 104,50 area after it traded 103,19 the lowest since March.
