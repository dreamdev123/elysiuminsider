Newsletter - Portfolio Journal - Feb 1, 2021

ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 1st of FEBRUARY 2021


The Past & Highlights

The stock market had their largest weekly loss in three months last week as volatile trading in certain parts of the market continued, raising concerns about speculative excesses.  The US GDP for Q4 revealed a moderate growth after the Q3 record growth. Indicating that consumers were more cautious with regards to their spending. Meanwhile business investments and housing were relatively strong. The outlook consensus indicates that growth should slow somewhat further this quarter, but activity and employment should improve especially since the 900 billion USD fiscal stimulus was approved in December.



S&P500 daily chart
 

Outlook

The overall fundamentals for the stock market have not changed despite of a negative return in the equity markets for January.  Major economies are still likely to gain momentum later this year, sparked by the mix of central-bank and government stimulus. 

The S&P500 tested the support around the 3700 area. It held during Friday’s trading session but is likely to be tested again. It is maybe the deciding point if we will see a little larger correction down to the 3600 area or not.
 

EA Portfolio Performance

Elysium AURUM DIGITAL portfolio EA can summarize a healthy January with a total net return of around 2,06% (adjusted for performance fees). The main positive contributions came from the majors EURUSD and GBPJPY followed by XAUUSD (spot gold). Eight different pairs were traded in January, where most of them contributed positively exempt from small losses in NZDJPY, NZDUSD, EURCAD and USDSGD. Best contributing currency pairs was EURUSD and USDCAD followed by EURCAD while the main trading activity was in EURUSD and EURJPY representing around 18 respectively 13 percentage of the volume for January. Elysium ALPHA LINEA portfolio EA gained a net return of around 1,65% for January. Main contributions came from USDCAD, NZDUSD and XAUUSD, while the most traded cross was EURUSD which represented around 12 percent of the traded volume. There are currently no figures reported yet for February.
 

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The last week of January was very volatile for the foreign exchange (FX) market. GameStop has been topping the headlines but there has been some news from a macro point of view as well. US stimulus, Central Banks and vaccine roll out disruptions etc.  British Pound (GBP) ended the week as the strongest one among the majors despite bets on negative interest rate from Bank of England (BoE). The same story goes for New Zealand Dollar (NZD) too, which ended as second strongest. 

The US dollar also had a strong week and increased against most of the other major currencies, and the Aussie dollar (AUD) ended as worst performing while Canadian Dollar (CAD) was somewhat supported by stronger than expected GDP data. Japanese Yen was surprisingly the second worst the past week despite of the late risk aversion sentiment in financial markets.
 


EURUSD daily chart
 

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) had overall a sideways consolidation week, traders are looking in the dark for direction. The precious metal trades right under the 50-day Exponential Moving Average (EMA) and has support from the raising trend line from early December. Maybe the downwards consolidation soon can be over, and we will slowly continue upwards again. 



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart 
 

Cryptos 
 
Monday the past week Bitcoin (BTCUSD) raised towards the 35 000 USD level before hitting reverse, never touched the main resistance level of around 37 175 USD. Then BTC fall to Wednesday intra-week low of around 28 283 USD. During Thursday Ilon Musk and Reddit’s co-founder Alexis Ohanian tagged Bitcoin in their profiles biographies which gave massive support and during Friday bitcoin rallied to its intra-week high of around 38 710 USD.  Overall Bitcoin gained close to around 10% the past week. Other interesting news the past week with regards to Bitcoin is that Canadian investment firm Ninepoint Partners completed the 180 million USD initial public offering of its cryptocurrency fund which is now fully invested. This plays well into the theme of Bitcoin becoming more and more widely accepted as an asset class for a wider range of investors.
