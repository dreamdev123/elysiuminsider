The Past & Highlights

Global stock markets were mixed the past week, but the Friday stock market rally erased any losses from the early week. Dow Jones outperformed Nasdaq the past week, and in the broader S&P500 index technology shares underperformed along with consumer discretionary, utilities, and real estate stocks while value stocks outperformed their growth counterparts for the third week in a row.

In general, the earnings season has been relatively “calm” in the sense that earnings beats or misses do not appear to have had dramatic short-term shock effects on stock prices.

Currency Markets and Outlook

US dollar (USD) was weak during last week and ended lower versus most other majors, as the huge non-farm payroll miss gave a strong node to Fed’s patient approach. The Federal Reserve is currently not able to even consider tapering and the continued expectation that the loose monetary policy is here to stay for longer boosted US stocks to a new record high. Japanese Yen (JPY) and Euro was the next weakest, and the New Zealand dollar (NZD) headed the other commodity currencies upwards. The Aussie dollar (AUD) was a little weaker due to the increased trade related tension with China.

EURUSD was initially weak and then reversed on Thursday to be very bullish. In the wave count then the second wave down (2?) was very small. Much smaller than expected, but the general Elliott Wave theory now implies, that since we crossed the former top of wave one (1?) we should now be in the third wave now. Which in theory should be the longest wave (3?), signaling that EURUSD should continue to be bullish trough out the week at least if the theory works, and the wave count is correct.

Commodities 
 

Spot gold (XAUUSD) had a very strong last week and regained its upwards momentum. That gold now trades above 1800 USD/ounce is very significantly bullish from a technical view. Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) is up for the second week in a row, the past week by around 3% (ending 9th of May). The previous week Bitcoin increased by around 15%. It has been a mixed week, first up during Monday then reversing to an intra-week low during Wednesday to around 52 960 USD. Then reversing again climbing to the intra-week high on Saturday at 59 450 USD.  The past week Ethereum (ETH) has been extremely bullish, driven by fundamental factors such as increased recognition from Visa and the European Investment Bank for example. It is likely that the recent bull market in ETH can spillover also on Bitcoin in the near term. Since institutions now trade portfolios with major cryptos and tends to rebalance their exposures from time to time.
