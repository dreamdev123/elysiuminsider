ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 23rd of NOVEMBER 2020
 

The Past & Highlights

The past week was a mixed balance between positive vaccine news and ever-growing restrictions aimed at curbing the recent spike in virus cases and hospitalizations. The shift from technology stocks into more cyclical stocks continued, as the vaccine developments improved investor sentiment and confidence about next year's outlook. Job-statistics showed worse than expected initial jobless claims, and that confirms that the economic recovery is likely to be slow before ultimately returning to pre-pandemic levels. 
 
News that the Treasury department is not going to extend certain funding for the Fed's lending facilities established earlier in the pandemic is catching attention, but it should probably not be interpreted as a signal that monetary-policy support will be significantly or permanently scaled back. The S&P500 was down around 0,77% during the past week. 



S&P 500 daily chart
 

Outlook

Technology sector and stocks that benefited from the "stay-at-home" trend have outperformed and lead the market rally since the March stock market bottom. The vaccine  development news has been a trigger for a larger scale market rotation where cyclical sectors, like energy and financials, which have been negatively impacted by the pandemic and are more sensitive to the reopening of the economy, outperformed, while sectors that have benefited from the pandemic, like technology and communication services instead have started to lag. This theme will probably go on for some time more if there are no negative setbacks on the vaccine front.

Currency Markets
 
FX markets, as well as gold and stocks were overall in consolidation mode last week.  Vaccine news kept the markets within their range and did not give the market a boost. Traders was probably waiting more confirmations that the corona will slow down and that the vaccines will be approved. USD was overall weak last week followed by the Swiss Franc (CHF). JPY, yen and the “kiwi dollar” NZD together with the British pound GBP where among the currencies that appreciated during last week. 
EURUSD is consolidation in the upwards trading channel 1,19 – 1,20 area.
 


EURUSD 2 years daily chart
 

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) was slightly down the past week and the key support around 1848 USD/ounce was intact. It is trading below its 55-day EMA (exponential moving average) of around 1896,5. Gold is obviously still in a larger consolidation phase and a break upwards are slightly more likely.
