Newsletter - Portfolio Journal - March 22, 2021

ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 22nd of MARCH 2021


The Past & Highlights

It is approximately one year ago since the stock market bottom in the 2020 corona-crash. It was among the shortest bear market through history. Since then, stock prices, commodities and long-term yields have increased all reflecting a more positive outlook for the global economy. The main triggers for the financial markets have been the step-by-step reopening of the economies, extreme fiscal spending, and widespread central bank stimulus.  Probably these measures will continue to support equity markets throughout the year. 

Overall, the stock market continued to print record levels in the early week but then started to decline while bond yields reached their highest levels in over a year. The energy sector declined sharply as oil prices saw their biggest daily drop since the summer, mainly triggered by the rising US inventories and demand concerns due to renewed lockdowns and the slow vaccine rollout in Europe. Banking stocks mainly was supported through the week by the increase in yields which is expected to support the lending margins and profitability.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
US treasury yields increased last week due to the FOMC statements from FED. Also concerns of the development of corona infections rate and the development of vaccinations worried traders somewhat. But there were not any extreme concerns. Japanese yen (JPY) Swiss Franc (CHF) and US dollar was the winners over the week. Among weaker currencies was Euro and sterling (GBP).   

GBPUSD also referred to as “cable” has been very volatile since BREXIT but moved in a strong upwards trend since April last year. Recently the 1,42 area was tested which is very close to the top from April 2018 where the cross was clearly meeting resistance. It is likely that there is a lot of take- profit-orders, buy-the-break, sell-orders, and stop-loss-orders around that level and not all of them was taken out in February. So, would not be surprised if we see another higher test of this area before any real correction to the downside can potentially start for GBPUSD.

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) had a pretty good week and trended slowly upwards over the last week. Gold felt some pressure from the increase of the longer-term treasury yields from the middle of the week. It has lost a little momentum in Monday’s trading session, but it is likely to continue upwards a little more since it still could be viewed as oversold from a technical perspective. To confirm the gold correction is over we would prefer to see a close above the 1800 USD/ounce level in the short to medium term perspective. Fundamentally some stabilizing of the US-treasury yields would be very supportive for gold.



XAUUSD daily chart (with MACD-indicator)
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) had an overall calm week. In the beginning of the week bitcoin plunged a little during last Monday. But after a very strong weekend. Then the flagship crypto went up sharply during Wednesday’s trading session, consolidated for the remaining of the week and lost a little over the weekend.  Looking at the news flow, it was also mixed. India will propose a ban on crypto currencies. Probably since no one want to hold on to Rupees for their savings due to inflation expectations, and since the country anyway has currency restrictions this is no surprise.  On the other side co-founder and former CEO of crypto exchange BTCC, Bobby Lee told CNBC that Bitcoin is in extreme bullish trend now and could surge to 300 000 USD. He based this forecast on historical patterns based on four-year cycles. But he also mentioned that after the target is reached there could be a larger correction.
