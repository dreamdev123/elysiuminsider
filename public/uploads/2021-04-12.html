Newsletter - Portfolio Journal - April 12, 2021

ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 12th of APRIL 2021


The Past & Highlights

Major US-indices moved steadily higher to record highs the past week, although the small-cap Russell 2000 Index recorded a modest loss. Tech focused Nasdaq outperformed the broad market S&P 500 Index but stayed below its February peak. Apple and Microsoft lifted both indices which together account for roughly 40% of the sector’s market capitalization. Casino and cruise line stocks was very strong while energy stocks were weak due to lowered oil prices. Growth stocks outperformed value stocks, narrowing the performance gap for the year to date. But in general, it was a rather quit week in the equity market.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
Swiss Franc (CHF) and Euro was the strongest majors last week, since many investors reversed their short positions accumulated in Q1. This also had an impact on sterling (GBP) which was the weakest among the majors the past week. Risk-appetite was back in the stock market, but it did not really reflect into the forex market and there was not any selling in Japanese Yen (JPY) nor massive buying in more risky commodity related currencies. Aussie Dollar (AUD) was among the weakest majors together with the US Dollar (USD). Canadian dollar was somewhat mixed, mainly due to some pressure in oil prices but gained support by the strong job-market report. 

EURUSD was strong the past week, the cross increased by almost 1,5%. Maybe still a little early to say that the downwards A-B-C correction on the wave count is over. But it looks more likely that we are then not. So, the pair is likely to continue upwards a little more before we see a smaller wave down (smaller than the previous upwards wave).

Commodities 
 
Spot gold (XAUUSD) had a strong past week and now show some strength. It looks like traders and investors now see gold as “buy dips”. The double bottom formation from March around 1677 USD/ounce serve as a strong support as well as a bullish signal. We just looking for a trend change in Exponential Moving Averages 50/200 (EMA) to confirm a trend change in the longer term. 



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart with MACD & EMA 50/200
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) had an overall strong week. Started last Monday upwards trading around 59200 USD by the end of the day and then starting to lose its momentum over Tuesday to finally print an intraweek low during Thursday around 55800 USD. After the low point at Thursday Bitcoin started to gain momentum again and traded up to around 60900 USD during Saturday. 

In North America, Canada has taken the lead on bitcoin ETFs (Exchange traded Funds). But currently there is nine active applications on the US Securities and Exchange Commission’s desk. Recently Galaxy who already is listed at the Toronto Stock Exchange filed for registration of an Bitcoin ETF. Overall a listed ETF in US is likely to be another milestone for the crypto and could be a trigger for a new all-time-high since the ETFs initially most likely will be a long-only tracker.
