ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 8th of FEBRUARY 2021


The Past & Highlights

Stock market in US posted the best weekly gain since November the past week. Major indices reached new All-Time-Highs. Equity investors focused on vaccine distribution and more fiscal stimulus expectations. Oil traded up to the highest levels in more than a year, close to a 10% gain for the week. 

The January jobs report disappointed somewhat, as the US economy added 49 000 jobs in January, below the 100 000 expected. But the market participants still forecast a strong rebound later this year. The optimism is supported by a combination of a slightly better new corona-infection trend despite of new strains of the virus. A better trend in initial jobless claims, it has declined for the third consecutive week and consumer spending is supported by additional fiscal stimulus, accommodative central-bank policies, and the rollout of vaccines. All together bosting optimism!

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The USD was the strongest major currency the past week, lost some of its strength on the release of Friday’s slightly disappointing non-farm payroll report. The Aussie Dollar (AUD) and New Zeeland dollar (NZD) had a very strong week, followed by sterling (GBP), euro, Swiss Franc, and the Japanese Yen (JPY) which was the weakest of the majors.   
    
EURUSD started the past week with a sharp decline closing around the lowest intraday level, a bearish sign which were followed by three more negative days. The past Monday’s sharp fall made the pair break out of the climbing trading channel and instead entering a falling trading channel. But there are many support levels between and around the 1,1750 and 1,1900 area. Therefore, it is difficult to see any clear direction for the pair short term. Breaking out of the falling channel should indicate some potential upside towards around 1,2170 area.

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) started the week down but got support from Friday´s weak job numbers. Technically gold is still in a correction phase waiting for clear direction. The longer-term trend for gold, represented by the EMA 200 (Exponential Moving Average) is still up, and was tested through the past week. So, there is a slightly optimism that we might have an interesting entry point around these levels as long as the longer-term trend remains upwards. 



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart with EMA 50/200 
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) rallied around 17% the past week, ending the 7th of February. Following on from a 2,50% gain from the previous week. During Monday the past week, Bitcoin had an intra-week low at 32 233 USD, and thereafter traded higher without any extreme volatility. During Saturday, the intra-week high was reached at 40 900 USD. 

Meanwhile a new fresh All-Time-High has been hit during Monday’s trading session.  Bitcoin was very bullish after that Tesla founder Elon Musk said that the company had invested 1,5 billion USD in Bitcoin recently. The car manufacturer also stated that the company may acquire and hold digital assets from time to time and that they aim to accept Bitcoin as a form of payment for its products in the future.
