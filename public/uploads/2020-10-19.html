Newsletter - Portfolio Journal Oct 19 - 2020

ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 19th of OCTOBER 2020
 

The Past & Highlights
 
Stock markets went slightly higher last week as traders continue to hope for a fiscal stimulus package. With regards to a vaccine against COVID19 some trials have been paused due to health concerns, and Pfizer has filed an emergency-use plan for the end of November. Retail sales are still the bright spot for the economy, showing an unexpected gain in September, even while jobless claims came in higher than expected and job growth showed signs of slowing.

The spread of Corona continues in Europe and new restrictions are almost announced daily, which risks dampening the economic activity. Also, in the US, the number of infected and hospitalized has started to rise again. Biden has expanded his lead in the presidential election in recent weeks and the market now sees lower volatility surrounding the election. 
 
Negotiations between Democrats and Republicans on a new financial support package is going slow. Federal Reserve more or less demands that politicians should lay party politics aside and agree to that all monetary policy efforts should not be in vain. The United States is still showing good signs of recovery, though some signs can be interpreted as the pace is decreasing. 
 
The Brexit negotiations are in its final stage, but yet there is no deal in place. Keep in mind that Boris Johnson said the country should get ready for a no-deal exit from the EU on December 31, in response to Brussels’ demands for more unilateral concessions. The uncertainly is very large at this point. 
 
The early part of earning season continues this week. Last week the winners were investment banks and retail banks were the losers. This week some of the really mega-cap companies report, such as IBM, Amazon, Netflix, Tesla, and Coca Cola for mentioning a few names.

Currency Markets

GBP, vs USD and EUR. There is currently high odds in favor of a key Brexit headline this or next week. Currently a positive headline is likely to merit a larger reaction than a negative one, as the latter could just mean a further delay of uncertainty rather than anything definitive that immediately points to a hard Brexit risk. With regards to GBPUSD the upside trigger is a close above 1,3000 that was tested, but rejected in early Monday morning trading. While the key support for the EURGBP is around 0,9000.

Commodities 

XAUUSD, gold is in a technical longer-term positive trend slowly seeking it´s way back to the 2000 USD level. Since August we have seen a correction from the early August all-time-high around the 2065 area. During the past couple of weeks, we have seen a consolidation phase that might be the preparation for a test of the 2000 level within this year. On the downside gold has support around the 1850 area followed by the 1800 area. In this time of uncertainty, the outlook for gold is somewhat more optimistic then bearish.
