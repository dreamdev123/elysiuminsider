ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 15th of MARCH 2021


The Past & Highlights

The stock market was bullish last week due to optimism over the economic recovery. The US S&P500 even printed a new record high.  A tame inflation report in conjunction with a halt in increase of the interest rates in US all contributed to the optimism. And in Europe ECB’s undertaking to accelerate asset purchases boosted risk appetite. But the major macro news was the passing of the American Rescue Plan (ARP) and the fact that US President Biden signed the pandemic-relief bill into law during Thursday. This round of stimulus was already to a very large extent anticipated by the financial markets so the focus will probably shift into following up on its actual delivery of economic results. 

The economy is already recovering but still there is large parts of the economy and labor force that still is not improving. While machine learning, automation, cloud computing, remote working has benefited from the pandemic, restaurants, cinemas and out of home entertainment have been suffering. The combination of the increase in productivity from the technology sector with the return of the suffering sectors is a great setup for a GDP boost during the second half of the year.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
Japanese Yen (JPY), the US dollar and the Swiss Franc (CHF) was the underperformers the past week. Global stocks had a rally supported by the stimulus in US and ECB’s promise of increased asset purchases.  The bullish major currencies the past week was Canadian dollar (CAD) supported by Bank of Canada (BOC) tapering and stronger job market data. Followed by Australia dollar (AUD) and The British pound (GDP). All in line with the risk-on theme in the financial markets. 

We saw a bounce back in EURUSD during the past week. From here we now test the bottom in the short-term upwards trading channel. Would not be surprised if it breaks downwards to test support around the 1,1750 area before the recoil is finalized. 

It is also Interesting to take a longer technical view of EURUSD and applying a wave count according to the Elliott Wave theory. It can then be interpreted as we are still in the semi-longer-term bull-market with five previous waves count upwards, with a traditional ABC recoil formation in progress or about to be finalized, which then in theory could be followed by a new potential five waves upwards count (if of course none of the exemptions occurs). If this theory applies, EURUSD looks very interesting for buying dips and start scaling in longer positions.

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) had an upwards move last week since bond yields took it easier. There has been a considerable correction for gold since the previous highs in early January.  We see an interesting bullish formation in the MACD indicator that indicates that we will see gold continuo higher the next couple of weeks (the red signal line crossed by the blue MACD line). Fundamentally this looks realistic if bond yields now goes into a slower steadier phase. Don’t have to decline but at least not rallies higher.  Short term view on gold therefore is increasingly more optimistic.

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) again had another strong week. The flagship crypto started last Monday’s trading session with a 2,9 percent gain closing around 52 270 USD. Fundamentally supported by the news that Norwegian Industrial Investment company Aker ASA will invest around 58 million USD in innovative crypto projects. Meanwhile also Stone Ridge Holdings Group, Morgan Stanley, New York Life, MassMutual, Soros Fund Management, and FS Investments announced a 200 million USD investment in Bitcoin company NYDIG. Overall Bitcoin had a steady upwards trading trend over all business week printing an intra-week high on Sunday just below 62 000 USD.
