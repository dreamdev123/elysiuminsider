ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 12th of OCTOBER 2020
 

The Past & Highlights
 
Equites went up last week, with the S&P 500 printing the best weekly gain since early July, while long-term government yields rose to a four-month high. The factor behind the stock-market strength was the anticipation that another stimulus package will eventually be passed despite the unsuccessful negotiations so far. The White House increased its fiscal stimulus offer to 1,8 trillion from 1,6 trillion USD, which partly bridges the gap but is still short of the 2,2 trillion USD package the House has already approved. A deal for further relief is likely, even if it ends up arriving after the election, as both sides agree on the need for more spending to offset the pandemic's hit to households and companies.

Looking forward, election turmoil and headlines will be key focus points for the market over the next couple of weeks. The stock market saw a lift last week as President Trump's health situation improved, but the contentious campaign and also the different wings within the republicans are likely to spur tension ahead.

Currency Markets

October 15th is getting closer and is set as the “deadline” in Brexit talks, but it appears that talks will continue past that date.  Since the US President Donald Trump has returned to the White House after getting ill from COVID19 he tweeted that fiscal stimulus talks were off, but on Friday, there were rumors in the market that Trump agreed to a deal. The situation remains uncertain. We also have natural disasters, such as pandemics and hurricanes this week.  Number of COVID19 cases continue to increase in the world, after slowing somewhat over the summer.  Local lockdowns have occurred, and more are imminent.  Hurricanes (and strikes) have helped oil climb to its highest levels since June around 40,9 USD (Light Sweet Crude) and we now see the start of the earnings season, with for example Alcoa set to report after market close on the 14th this week. 

EURUSD, the pair increased by around 1% during the past week. The dollar was week and the risk appetite was back on. From technical perspective the EURUSD are in a positive trend since may, but we have been in a consolidation phase since end of July. From a longer perspective a break of the former top in early September around 1,201 would indicate that the EUR could gain more versus the USD.

Commodities 

XAUUSD, gold traded through its resistance around 1920 USD during Friday and closed close to the 1930 area. The bullish run cold be explained to some extent by USD weakness due to increased risk appetite from investors and traders. Also, the 10-year yields were under pressure. Looks like gold is set up for an interesting week, looks more bullish then bearish.
