ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 30th of NOVEMBER 2020


The Past & Highlights

The past week continued to be positively impacted by coronavirus-vaccine news, which helped move the Dow Jones up more than 12% for the month, crossing the 30 000 mark for the first time, and on-track to have the best monthly gain since January 1987. It US colleagues the S&P500 and Russell 2000 both posted fresh record highs as well. Was a light calendar last week in terms of data releases so focus was around increasing number of COVID-19 cases and hospitalizations and positive vaccine news.



Dow Jones Index Daily Chart - An Incredible Recovery!
 

Outlook

It is a remarkable recovery that we have seen in the equity markets since the pandemic-panic during the spring. And is it still reason to be optimistic? The rally has been driven by the rebound from the depths of the pandemic crisis, with the latest boost coming from encouraging vaccine news.
 
In terms of the future, there are reasons to be optimistic but realistic. We will so more challenges going forward in terms of shutdowns and negative effects of the pandemic in various regions, but the stock-market gains are reflecting expectations for a post-vaccine large scale economic rebound next year and forward. This means that 2021 needs to deliver in terms of killing of COVID19 and economies needs to grow if equity markets should continue to perform. Probably we will see more volatility during next year, but it is likely that the stock market will stay strong in December if nothing negative is presented on the vaccine front.

Currency Markets
 
As mentioned before vaccine optimism moved global stocks higher last week, and commodity currencies ended generally higher. The kiwi (New Zeeland Dollar) was the best performer while Yen and US Dollar ended as the worst performing ones. Though, no key levels were taken out, except in NZDUSD and NZDJPY. Sterling (British Pound) ended as just the third weakest and there was a late selloff during the last week, probably triggered by the Brexit transition period that is going to end in a month’s time. It is likely that we will see an increase in volatility in GBP during the next weeks especially towards its European majors. 

EU negotiation team are currently in London for a four day make-or break Brexit negotiation. With just a month before the end of transition period. Either way, an announcement of a deal or a no-deal would probably trigger fast price action in GBP. 

EURGBP has been extremely volatile since the Brexit election and is now still trading in a downwards trading channel in a daily chart. Maybe we will see an upwards break out of this channel?

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) broke its key support around 1847 USD/ounce during the past weeks trading. Safe heaven assets were clearly out of fashion when the risk on apatite got a vaccine boost. Support is seen around 1750 to 1680 area. The longer terms fundamentals are still intact for gold, so we view this more as an interesting longer-term opportunity for adding gold to a diversified portfolio.  But from a short-term trading point of view, we thrive from volatility and longer term direction is of less importance.
