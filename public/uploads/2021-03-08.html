Newsletter - Portfolio Journal - March 8, 2021

ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 8th of MARCH 2021


The Past & Highlights

The battle between interest rates and the stock market continued last week. Yields come up a little and the stock market went down a little.  Investors decided that there is a conflict between higher equity prices and higher yields and that they cannot coexist. Meanwhile the interest-rates are a little higher, but still very low. In fact, the ten-year US treasury yield raised sharply last week, but it is simple back at the same level as around one year ago. Overall, this is more indicating the market anticipate that the economy gradually moves back to pre-pandemic levels. There was a temporary shift in focus last week from reopening and stimulus to inflation and interest-rate risks.

With regards to the pandemic, it is likely that we have seen the start of another wave in Europe and the US during March due to the more contagious British strain taking over followed by improvement again in April when spring arrives. But hopefully and most likely mortality rates will drop in the second quarter as elderly population becomes vaccinated.

EA Portfolio Performance

Elysium AURUM DIGITAL portfolio EA continued to perform the past week and is currently up net of around 0,93% (adjusted for performance fees) for March, month to date. The main contributors were from trading in the pairs EURUSD and spot silver (XAGUSD). Looking at commodities, spot gold (XAUUSD) also contributed positively to the performance for March and is currently the most traded pair with around 35% of the traded volume so far. 

Elysium PINNACLE portfolio EA have continued positively and is now up net by around 0,15% (adjusted for performance fees) month to date. In March, the portfolio has mainly traded USDCAD and EURUSD representing around 17% respectively 15% of the volume followed by EURCAD representing around 13% of the traded volume in March. Other pairs traded frequently were USDJPY, GBPUSD and EURJPY. Trading has been spread over 9 different pairs so far in March. Best risk/reward attribution was from the major pairs EURUSD followed by GBPUSD so far for the current month.
 

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The increasing US yields was focus last week and the US dollar closed over all higher after FED Chairman Jerome Powell’s indifferent comments on yield. The impression was that he was not concerned with regards to the recent increase in US treasury yields. The strongest among the majors was the Canadian dollar (CAD) supported by oil prices. On the other side was Swiss Franc (CHF), Japanese Yen (JPY) and the Euro. The upcoming ECB meeting the 11th of March can put more pressure on the downside for the Euro. 

Was a sharp drop in the EURUSD pair last week. The major support area around 1,1750 should be significant. Maybe can be a point for a bounce back upwards while we wait and look for a longer-term direction for the pair.
 


EURUSD daily chart
 

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) closed lower for the third consecutive week and took out the June 5th, 2020 bottom around USD 1704. A significant difference this time compared with last year when central banks and governments were implementing massive amounts of monetary and fiscal stimulus and FED was flooding the market with liquidity at the start of the pandemic is that this time the increasing treasury yield has complicated it for gold. Meanwhile this could be viewed from fundamental aspects as a shorter-term price adjustment. There appears to many longer-term investors that see value in gold around this price levels and that the recall soon might be over.



XAUUSD daily chart
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) had another strong week. Trading above 52 000 USD during Wednesday as an intraweek high, bouncing back slightly during Thursday and then closing right below 52 000 USD in the weekend.  
 
Some interesting news through the past week that is worth mentioning is that Hong Kong-listed Meitu which operates in the image and video processing software industry, announced March the 7th the purchase and 17,9 million USD of bitcoin (BTC) and 22 million USD in Ether (ETH). This is another indication that larger corporations now add the major crypto currencies in its treasury portfolios.
