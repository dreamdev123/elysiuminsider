Newsletter - Portfolio Journal - Jan 18, 2021

ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 18th of JANUARY 2021


The Past & Highlights

The US stock market took a break from the recent rally and was down around 1% after the all-time-high from prior week.  Earnings season has started, and JP Morgan and Wells Fargo reported earnings last week. Earnings per share was slightly better than expected, but revenue was slightly lower for Wells Fargo while JP Morgan exceeded expectations on both parameters. But the financial markets were not so impressed and both stocks traded slightly down. The next US president Joe Biden announced a 1,9 trillion dollar fiscal-stimulus plan that aims to counter the effects of covid19.  Vaccine distributions are not totally in line with expectations and new coronavirus strains being detected and therefore investors are starting to get worried that economic lockdowns could be here to stay for a while longer. US economic data indicates that the job market is once again under stress, as initial claims saw a spike for the week ending January 9. 



S&P500 daily chart
 

Outlook

There are three major focus points that concerns investors going forward: 
1) Rising inflation expectations for the first time in many years. Rising commodity prices and a weak dollar has sent the US 10-Year treasury bond yield to the highest since 2018. Only the weak labor market offset the raising yield expectations to some extent.
2) Additional fiscal support is expected since the first Biden plan that was announced last week is expected to be followed up by another one. The next plan might include additional checks to households, expanded unemployment benefits, a minimum-wage increase, vaccine funding, and increased aid to state and local governments. 
3) Financial markets are generally estimating no major setbacks of the distribution of covid19 vaccines, and therefore the economy should return closer to its normal state by year-end.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
British Pond (GBP) was a top performer last week despite of a selloff by the end of the week versus Yen (JPY) and US dollar (USD). Among weak currencies last week was Euro, New Zealand Dollar (NZD), and to a lesser extent the Australian dollar (AUD). Overall, the currency markets were mixed, and Joe Biden’s new stimulus package did not create any extraordinary currency price action. 

EURUSD fall back in the trading channel during last week and found support around 1,2060 level. It is likely to see some bonce upwards from here. If continue to go down the 1,20 level would be a key level to follow.

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) had a somewhat boring week and appears to be in a consolidation phase mostly rangebound trading as traders continued to search for a bullish catalyst to drive prices higher. Naturally, gold is an investment that competes with stocks, bonds, and currencies, or basically any asset with an expected positive return. The trigger can come at any time investors get a little risk averse, so the simple strategy is to always hold on to a little gold in the portfolio and increase the position when the price action is clearly in favor of the buyers. At some point fiscal and monetary stimulus probably will start to drive prices higher again.



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTCUSD) fell by around 6% in the week ending 17th January. A reverse from the approximately 16% rally from the previous week. Bitcoin closed the week around 35 900 USD. Monday last week was a bearish day for bitcoin, and it printed an intraweek low point of around 30 635 USD while it rallied to around 40 001 USD during Wednesday before reversing again. 
 
Regarding the fundamental aspects of bitcoin and cryptos there is getting more and more signs that bitcoin getting more and more accepted as an asset class within the high street banks. Goldman Sachs head of commodities Jeff Currie says to CNBC that Bitcoin is starting to mature but that intuitional money still is a tiny fraction of the market. But the more money that goes into bitcoin the more stable the market will become. CNBC also reported that Goldman Sachs has issued a request for information (RFI) to explore digital asset custody, according to a source inside the bank and that JP Morgan and Citi Bank already has done so. Overall, this could be the starting point for Bitcoin to be a larger and more widely accepted asset class within institutional asset management.
