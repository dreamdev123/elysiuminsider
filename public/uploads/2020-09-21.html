ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 21th of SEPTEMBER 2020
 

The Past & Highlights
 

Mixed week for stocks as value outperforms
 
Equities was mixed last week, with merger news and some renewed COVID-19 vaccine optimism seemingly offset by worries that the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy was becoming less effective in supporting the recovery. Especially value stocks and small-caps outperformed, while traders and investors continued to sell off internet and information technology giants that had led the market in recent months. 

Energy stocks outperformed within the S&P 500 Index, helped by a large and unexpected drawdown in domestic oil inventories and Saudi Arabia’s efforts to force production cuts by other major oil exporters. Communication services sector were among the worst performers, pushed down by Facebook due to reports that the Federal Trade Commission was preparing antitrust action against the company. Quadruple witching (the expiry of multiple equity derivatives) during Friday cased volatility to increase.  The benchmark 10-year U.S. Treasury bond increased somewhat during the past week.  

World Health Organization (WHO) warned that Europe is facing a "very serious" situation as the number of new coronavirus cases reported weekly in Europe hit 300 000 for the first time. France has reported the highest number of daily coronavirus cases since May and in the UK, the number of new infections almost doubled to 6000 per day. Bank of England (BoE) also warned that coronavirus and a hard Brexit could slow the economic recovery. 

This week's main focus will be the Purchasing Managers' Index, which is presented from a number of different countries, including the United States, the United Kingdom, the euro area and Japan. The index has recovered from the plunge during the spring, but as the spread of infection in the euro area increases again, it will be interesting to see if the sentiment among purchasing managers is negatively affected.

Currency Markets

USD, On Wednesday, Fed Chairman Jerome Powell will be testifying to the House of Representatives’ Select Subcommittee on the Coronavirus Crisis. In prior interviews, he has expressed the need for continued monetary support with a growing emphasis on the fiscal side. Powell has stated that monetary policy can only do so much and indicated the need for the federal government to step in. Republicans and Democrats have been locked in a deadlock with regards to the proposal for another fiscal stimulus package with the former wanting a trimmed down version after the latter’s $3 trillion aid bill passed in May. So far, the market reaction to the lack of progress has been somewhat wait and see. Since there remain several significant risks to the economic outlook, modest signs of stabilization appear to be increasing risk appetite and punishing demand for low-risk assets.
 
EURUSD 30 minutes 5 days chart
 

GBP, will likely continue to be under siege by the ongoing Brexit negotiations ahead of a key deadline in the fall. Recently the tension has escalated after Prime Minister Boris Johnson introduced the Internal Market Bill that overrides key points set in the original EU-UK Withdrawal Agreement; one of them being the Northern Ireland Protocol. The bill’s main aim is to ensure the fluid movement of goods between the four nations of the United Kingdom. The likelihood of a hard Brexit has now increased severely.

Commodities 

XAUUSD, Gold hit the 50-Day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at $1938 for the second time this month and can be considered to be range bound following the limited reaction to the Federal Reserve interest rate decision. Shorter term scalping strategies applies waiting break out of the triangle for direction. Longer term fundamental view of gold is more bullish since it is considered a safe heaven and protects against inflation.
