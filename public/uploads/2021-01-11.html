ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 11th of JANUARY 2021


The Past & Highlights

Stock markets had a strong start of the year globally and many major indexes printed fresh record highs last week. The democrat party won the Senate majority following a pair of narrow runoff victories in Georgia and lead the path for more fiscal stimulus later in the year to aid the recovery. Democrat´s control also increases the likely hood of higher corporate tax rates but considering the small majority and current state of the economy, tax reforms might not be a priority in 2021. The ten-year US government bond yield was above 1% for the first time in nine months, while small-cap and international stocks outperformed, signaling optimism for the future for the post-vaccine phase. Saudi Arabia unexpectedly announced a production cut and WTI oil rose above 50 USD for the first time since February. 



S&P500 daily chart
 

Outlook

We saw some unfortunate political and social events last week but from a financial markets perspective economic data revealed a mix of resiliency and lockdown stress. Political uncertainties captured the headlines, and the stock market continued higher as investor focus in policy stimulus and a post-vaccine recovery. The stock market remained focused and took its energy from fundamentals such as economy, corporate profits, and interest rates. Generally, US stock markets perform well above average the year after a presidential election. The combination of increased corporate earnings, stimulus and low interest rates will support equities in 2021.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
The market participants were all in risk seeking mode last week. Despite of surges in coronavirus infection and death, return to strict lockdowns and chaos in Washington. USD had an overall slightly weak week but with some support from the selloff in gold and the increase in US treasury yields. Also, JPY (Japanese Yen) and GBP had a week performance while AUD (Australian dollar) and NZD (New Zealand dollar) had a strong week. 

EURUSD has traded in a longer upwards trend since Q2 2019. The trend is still intact, so will be interesting to follow if this bullish trend will change during this year. 
 


EURUSD daily chart
 

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) had a volatile week and first traded up until the middle of last week and then went down reaching towards the 1880 USD level, but then pulled back. Traders has been somewhat confused when it comes to stimulus in the US, and probably when the outline is clearer the longer-term buyers will line up again. The short-term pullback could be seen as an advantage for longer term buyers to scale up their positions.



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart
 

Cryptos 
 
Bitcoin (BTC) volatility increased over the weekend when a wave of profit taking sent the cryptocurrency market lower. Bitcoin set a new all-time high during Friday’s trading session and printed prices around USD 42 000 to start the week trading around USD 32 000. The bounce back in Bitcoin could be an interesting opportunity since some of the bounce back also could be explained by the futures market where traders had leveraged their positions and then liquidate positions due to higher weekend margins, which result in massive selloff within a small timeframe where natural buyers are not present. This provided an excess supply in short time when demand is naturally low causing a fast price drop. According to data from CoinMarketCap, trading volume in Bitcoin exceeded 100 billion USD in the last 24 hours. Ethereum (ETH) fell simultaneously from 1 350 USD to just over 1 000 USD before consolidating somewhat.
