ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 2nd of NOVEMBER 2020
 

The Past & Highlights

Focus on US Elections and Corona shutdowns
 
The US presidential election are finally here this Tuesday, and the major polls have Joe Biden in the lead by various amounts.  But the financial markets remember what happened in 2016 when Donald Trump beat Hillary Clinton after the polls had her ahead leading into the election. This certainly ad some element of uncertainty.

Covid19 cases continues to grow in Europe and the US as national restrictions are put into place in Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Ireland, among others. Last week, the Bank of Canada (BOC), Bank of Japan (BOJ) and the European Central Bank (ECB) came out overall in line with the market’s expectations, except for an extremely dovish ECB.

During last week global equites fell somewhat. The main trigger for the sell-off was news that daily coronavirus cases have hit new record highs, and by less certainty that we will see another round of fiscal stimulus in US this year. The tech sector in US that has been outperforming this year was down around 6,5% last week which makes it one of the worst sectors the past week. On the positive macro side was the strong third-quarter US GDP growth, a labor market that is continuing to recover, and consumer spending that is continuing to exceed expectations.

Currency Markets and Outlook

This is perhaps one of the most important election in US history and the market fears an unclear US president election outcome. There is expected to be a high participation rate in the election and that it may take time before we have a result. What makes the election even more special is that the current pandemic entails some restrictions on voters' opportunities to vote. A much larger number than usual will vote by mail, which in turn means that it will take much longer time to count all votes than in previous elections. The increase of postal votes is also considered to benefit Biden and has therefore provoked dissatisfied comments from President Trump. He has already indicated that a victory for Joe Biden means that there could have been electoral fraud. If there is an even election where Donald Trump is the winner, it is also reasonable to expect Biden to question the election result, but with a Republican-dominated Supreme Court, it should not stand in the way of Trump. 
 
If the election outcome is somehow perceived as unclear it will probably fuel the contradictions and the risk of more violent riots is obvious. Of course, it also affects the economy's ability to recover from the deep slump caused by the pandemic. But above all, an extended election process means that a new support package can take even longer and that volatility in the financial markets could increase. 
 
EURUSD has traded out of the positive range and broke the support around 1,1741 during the 29th of October. The euro was mainly sold off because of the Covid-19 lockdowns in Europe. The US futures markets indicates that there is a large long speculative positioning in the pair, this can cause even faster moves if the stops are triggered. The pair has support around 1,1600 area if that breaks the next level on the downside is 1,1500 followed by the 1,1400-1450 area.
