ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 21st of DECEMBER 2020


The Past & Highlights

The stock market went higher last week, with all major U.S. indexes hitting fresh all-time highs. Fiscal-stimulus optimism, along with a brighter longer-term outlook driven by the rollout of vaccines, continued to support sentiment. The Longer-term Treasury yields increased modestly, and oil had its seventh straight weekly gain. Macro data were mixed, indicating that the economic recovery is losing some momentum due to rising virus cases and renewed restrictions in activity. Retail sales and job growth show weakness. A compromise has now been reached regarding a new fiscal stimulus package reported to be around 900bn USD of which 284bn is through the paycheck protection program (PPP). 
 
In Europe there was no Brexit deal reached during last week nor through the weekend and now the European Parliament cannot ratify the deal before the New Year. But the EU Council can provisionally apply the UK-EU treaty pending full ratification early next year. So, there is not expected to be a final decision before Christmas, but maybe before New Year.



S&P 500 daily chart
 

Outlook

Financial markets are split between the renewed spread of Covid-19 infections, especially with regards to the new strain of coronavirus from UK that now results in new lockdowns and travel bans where for example Germany, France, Italy, the Netherlands, and others have banned travel from the UK.  The positive news is the upcoming U.S stimulus package that investors wanted seems to be put in place. The stimulus targets especially smaller businesses, unemployed and households with cash distributions. Going forward, probably news regards the new more contagious UK strain of Corona, roll-out of vaccine in combination of economic harm done by lockdowns will be the focus for investors and traders the upcoming weeks.  In the stock market we might see the usual year end window dressing theme.
 

EA Portfolio Performance

Elysium AURUM DIGITAL portfolio EA is up net of around 2,46% (adjusted for performance fees) for December, month to date. The main contributors were from trading in NZDJPY and USDJPY. But most pairs contributed with positive returns exempt for the EURCAD and NZDUSD crosses, for the current month. 

Elysium PINNACLE portfolio EA had a good performance the past week and is now up net by around 4,34% (adjusted for performance fees) month to date. In December, the portfolio has mainly traded USDCAD representing around 18% of the volume followed by EURJPY and NZDJPY representing around 12% each of the traded volume in December. Other pairs traded frequently was EURCAD and EURUSD with approximately 10% each of traded volume in December. Best risk/reward attribution was from NZDJPY followed by USDCAD so far for the current month. 

After a slow start of the month the Elysium ALPHA LINEA portfolio EA had another very good week and are now up net by around 7,75% (adjusted for performance fees) for December, month to date. Trading in NZDJPY has mainly contributed to the return the past week, but also AUDJPY, GBPAUD and GBPJPY contributed to the positive performance. Mostly traded pairs were the majors EURUSD and GBPUSD with around 19% respectively 18% of the traded volume for December so far.
 

Currency Markets
 
The past week the overall USD down trend continued, but Canadian dollar (CAD) was even weaker. CAD has been supported by increasing oil prices, but traders decided to sell last week despite of raising oil price. Risk appetitive was in general strong last week with NASDAQ printing new all-time-high, this kept JPY and CHF soft last week. As Brexit trade negotiations went into an extra mile, Sterling (GBP) reversed some of prior week’s losses and ended as the strongest one the past week, but in the Monday morning session with a no Brexit deal during the weekend it is very weak. 

Since middle of march sterling (GBP) has been in a raising trend versus the dollar (USD). But due to uncertainty regarding the Brexit negotiations it is a very volatile cross. There is a significant resistance around the 1,35 area that was tested last week. It will be interesting to see if that holds or not, if holds maybe we might see a breakout on the downside of the trend soon.

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) broke above its previous local high after last week’s Wednesday’s FOMC Statement announcement. And gold had overall a strong week. Technically it is still bound in its falling trading channel from early august but is now testing the upper band.  A breakout could confirm that we go back into the more bullish longer-term trend. Maybe to initially test the 1950 USD/ounce level.
