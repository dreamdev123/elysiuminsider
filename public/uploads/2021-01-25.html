Newsletter - Portfolio Journal - Jan 25, 2021

ELYSIUM CAPITAL WEEKLY UPDATE the 25th of JANUARY 2021


The Past & Highlights

The US stock market closed the past week slightly off record highs, as investors continue to balance positive news with near-term challenges. Focus was the 1,9 trillion dollar fiscal-stimulus proposal from the Biden administration and the setback in coronavirus in conjunction with the vaccine rollout. Meanwhile the republicans are slightly skeptical to such a sizable stimulus plan, concerned about the long-term sustainability of the government's deficit levels. Earnings season has started and this week close to a quarter of the companies represented in S&P500 will report. Housing data continued to be strong, both permits and housing starts came out strong but there was a short-term pullback in the refinancing of mortgages.



S&P500 daily chart
 

Outlook

There has been a break in the sector rotation where cyclical companies have gained momentum at the expense of corona winners, but now there is once again a headwind for IT, communication services and non-durable goods. The reason is higher corona infection rates and new, more infectious strains of the coronavirus with stricter restrictions as a result. There is the scenario that the existing vaccines will not prove to be as effective against, for example the South African strain of corona virus. It is currently a lot of optimism in the stock prices so room for setbacks are very limited. Meanwhile 
Pfizer and Moderna have stated that it takes around 4-6 weeks to update the vaccine if necessary, but that would still mean that we risk having to vaccinate those who have already received the first vaccine again. Therefore, it is likely to see some more volatility going forward and that the rally will be halted for some time, at least until it is clearer that the vaccine campaign is successful.

Currency Markets and Outlook
 
Euro was surprisingly the strongest the past week followed by sterling (GBP) in a close call with the New Zealand Dollar. The US dollar and Yen were the worst performing currencies last week, mainly due to the risk seeking environment. Investors were focusing on the increasing US fiscal stimulus, as well as resolution of the pandemic with vaccinations despite near term lockdowns. The Canadian Dollar (CAD) was surprisingly among the weak currencies, as the balanced Bank of Canada did not give enough support, while oil price did not continue to increase. 

EURUSD, the support line in the upwards channel held and the expected price bounce upwards from around 1,2050 did come true. The price is likely to remain in the raising trading channel for the remaining of the week in a consolidation phase.
 


EURUSD daily chart
 

Commodities 
 
Gold (XAUUSD) fall back a bit during the trading session on Friday as the 50-day EMA (exponential moving average) has offered a significant amount of resistance. Currently the 200-day EMA around 1820 USD level should offer a significant support. But keep in mind that investors are keeping a close eye on the interest rates in the United States because if they start to rise is very likely that we will continue to see rotation towards the us dollar. Such a scenario could create some more short-term pressure on gold. Anyway, the longer-term view as inflation prevention remains intact therefore any push back is a longer term buy opportunity. 



XAUUSD (gold) daily chart with EMA 50/200
 

Cryptos 
 
BTCUSD, dropped around 10% in the week ending 24th of January. Following on from a 6% decline from the previous week, Bitcoin closed the week around 32 320 USD. Bitcoin started the week with increasing prices and traded around 37 937 USD during Tuesday’s trading session not touching its major resistance level at 40 389 USD. Then falling until Friday to around 29 300 USD level before it started to increase over the weekend. 
 
Fundamental aspects surrounding bitcoin continues to improve. Reuters reported that Bank of America's January survey of fund managers indicated that bitcoin is now seeing the most capital inflow. According to the survey, bitcoin has replaced “long tech” (bullish bets on major technology companies) as the most “crowded trade” – that is, the one seeing the most capital inflow. This is the first-time long tech been replaced from the top position since October. Also, notable is that the third most popular trade is to bet against the US dollar, according to Reuters. There are now several signs that Bitcoin is becoming increasingly more and more of an institutional asset class.
